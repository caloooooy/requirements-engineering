4 Major Phases of Requirements Engineering

1. Requirements Elicitation

⇾ Is the practice of researching and discovering the requirements of a system from users, customers, and other stakeholders.

2. Requirements Analysis

⇾ Focuses on the tasks that determine the needs or conditions to meet the new or altered product or project, taking account of the possibly conflicting requirements of the various stakeholders, analyzing, documenting, validating and managing software or system requirements.

3. Requirements Documentation

⇾ In project management describes how each requirement meets the business needs for the project. Requirements should be measurable, traceable, consistent, complete and acceptable to the stakeholders.

4. Requirements Verification & Validation

⇾ Are independent procedures that are used together for checking that a product, service, or system meets requirements and specifications and that it fulfills its intended purpose.

- means it is not clear who has the say, - who's allowed to state reqs, - not clear which stakeholders are the most important ones to interview - and that can later on lead into trouble, when you find out oh there another bunch of poips i need to talk to becsau i didn't coz i ddindt know

Communication

Moving Targets

- customer change their opinion about what they wanted - like last week they need these 3 features but the other one isn't that importatn anymore and just want the other two. then you say sure, did it then they changed their mind again - the problem is you are not moving forward in development and eventually you'll be runin over budget and overtime - your clients may always change their mind at some point - that's why you need to make sure they sign off on the base line req after a specified period of time and that its clear when they need to pay additional charges or when pay for an extra feature to avoid the cost of moving targets

Technically Unfeasible Requirements

- to deal with those make sure on every domain you are touching you have one domain expert on your team

Stake Holders don't know Requirements from previous solutions

- they are not experts on the domain of software systems development at least they are not usually - they dont necessarily knokw how to separate between what they knew as a previous solution and what is it they actually need - they doesn't know what they want until you show it to them - like when filing a documents every single day having a machine do it for you digitally will be so much improvements

Underspecified Requirements

- ex: you can only delete a post when logged in. after logging in the user tries to create a post he couldn't because it was said that you can only "delete when logged in" didn't specify that user can do several things when loged in
